Common Cyber Threats

Understanding the digital dangers that target individuals and organizations in today's interconnected world

Critical

Malware

Malicious software designed to infiltrate, damage, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems. Includes viruses, worms, trojans, and spyware.

Common Attack Vectors:

  • Email attachments and links
  • Infected downloads and software
  • USB drives and removable media
  • Compromised websites

High

Phishing

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as trustworthy entities in electronic communications, often through fake emails or websites.

Warning Signs:

  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Suspicious sender addresses
  • Generic greetings
  • Requests for personal information

Critical

Ransomware

Malicious software that encrypts files or locks computer systems, demanding payment for restoration. Often spreads through networks causing widespread damage.

Prevention Strategies:

  • Regular system backups
  • Updated security software
  • Employee training
  • Network segmentation

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation techniques used to trick people into divulging confidential information or performing actions that compromise security.

Common Techniques:

  • Pretexting and impersonation
  • Baiting with attractive offers
  • Tailgating and physical access
  • Authority and urgency tactics

DDoS Attacks

Distributed Denial of Service attacks overwhelm servers with traffic from multiple sources, making services unavailable to legitimate users.

Impact Areas:

  • Website and service availability
  • Business operations disruption
  • Revenue and reputation loss
  • Customer trust degradation

Critical

Data Breaches

Unauthorized access to confidential data, often resulting in theft of personal information, financial records, or intellectual property.

Common Causes:

  • Weak authentication systems
  • Unpatched software vulnerabilities
  • Insider threats and misuse
  • Poor data encryption practices
